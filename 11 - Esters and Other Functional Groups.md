## 1. Esters ($R-COOR'$)
- **Naming:** Two-part name:
  1. **Alkyl:** From the alcohol (part attached to $O$).
  2. **Alkanoate:** From the acid (part with $C=O$). Change *-oic acid* to **-oate**.
  - *Example:* *Ethyl ethanoate* ($CH_3COOCH_2CH_3$).
- **Properties:** Fruity smells. Polar but no H-bonding with self.

## 2. Ethers ($R-O-R'$)
- **Naming:** **Alkoxy + Alkane**.
  - Identify groups on both sides of $O$. The smaller is the *alkoxy* prefix; the larger is the *alkane* parent.
  - *Example:* *Methoxyethane* ($CH_3OCH_2CH_3$).
- **Properties:** Very unreactive. Excellent solvents.

## 3. Amines ($R-NH_2$)
- **Naming:** Suffix **-amine**.
  - If secondary/tertiary, name smaller groups with the prefix **N-** (e.g., *N-methylmethanamine*).
- **Properties:** Basic. Fishy odor.

## 4. Amides ($R-CONH_2$)
- **Naming:** Suffix **-amide**.
  - Replace *-oic acid* with *-amide* (e.g., *Ethanamide*).
- **Properties:** Contains the **peptide bond** (amide link) found in proteins.
