Alcohols ($R-OH$) are defined by the hydroxyl group.

## 1. Physical Properties
- **Hydrogen Bonding:** Highly polar $O-H$ bond leads to **high boiling points** and high **water solubility** (for short chains).
- **Classification:** Primary ($1^\circ$), Secondary ($2^\circ$), Tertiary ($3^\circ$).

## 2. IUPAC Nomenclature
Alcohols use the suffix **-ol**.

1. **Parent Chain:** Longest chain containing the $-OH$ carbon. Replace *-e* with *-ol*.
2. **Numbering:** Start from the end **closest to the $-OH$ group**. The $-OH$ group takes priority over alkyl groups and multiple bonds.
3. **Position:** Indicate the location of $-OH$ with a number (e.g., *propan-2-ol*).
4. **Unsaturated Alcohols:** If a double bond is present, the $-OH$ still gets the lowest possible number. Name as *alken-ol* (e.g., *prop-2-en-1-ol*).
5. **Cyclic Alcohols:** The $-OH$ carbon is **always Carbon 1**.

## 3. Synthesis
- **Hydration:** Alkene + $H_2O / H^+ \rightarrow$ Alcohol (Markovnikov).
- **Substitution:** Alkyl Halide + $NaOH \rightarrow$ Alcohol.
- **Fermentation:** Sugar $\xrightarrow{yeast}$ Ethanol + $CO_2$.

## 3. Reactions
- **Oxidation:**
  - $1^\circ \text{ Alcohol} \rightarrow \text{Aldehyde} \rightarrow \text{Carboxylic Acid}$
  - $2^\circ \text{ Alcohol} \rightarrow \text{Ketone}$
  - $3^\circ \text{ Alcohol} \rightarrow \text{No Reaction}$
- **Dehydration:** Alcohol $\xrightarrow{H_2SO_4, \Delta}$ Alkene + $H_2O$.
- **Combustion:** Alcohol + $O_2 \rightarrow CO_2 + H_2O$.
