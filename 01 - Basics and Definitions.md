Organic Chemistry is the study of **Carbon-based compounds**. Carbon's unique ability to form stable chains and rings (**catenation**) allows for the vast complexity of life.

## Why Carbon?
1. **Tetravalency:** Carbon has 4 valence electrons, allowing it to form 4 strong covalent bonds.
2. **Hybridization:** Carbon mixes its $s$ and $p$ orbitals ($sp^3, sp^2, sp$) to create specific 3D shapes:
   - **$sp^3$:** Tetrahedral ($109.5^\circ$) - Found in [[03 - Alkanes]].
   - **$sp^2$:** Trigonal Planar ($120^\circ$) - Found in [[04 - Alkenes and Alkynes#Alkenes|Alkenes]].
   - **$sp$:** Linear ($180^\circ$) - Found in [[04 - Alkenes and Alkynes#Alkynes|Alkynes]].

## 3. Representing Organic Molecules
Chemists use different ways to draw molecules, each showing a different level of detail:

1. **Structural Formula:** Shows every atom and every bond (e.g., $CH_4$ drawn with 4 lines to H).
2. **Condensed Formula:** Groups atoms together but keeps the main sequence (e.g., $CH_3CH_2CH_3$ for propane).
3. **Skeletal (Line-Angle) Formula:** The most common in organic chemistry.
   - Vertices and ends of lines represent **Carbon atoms**.
   - **Hydrogen atoms** attached to carbons are implied (assumed to fill the 4-bond requirement).
   - Heteroatoms (O, N, Cl, etc.) are always shown.

## 4. Homologous Series
A family of compounds with the same functional group and general formula.
- **Key Traits:** Members differ by a $-CH_2-$ unit, show a gradual change in physical properties (e.g., BP increases with mass), and have similar chemical properties.
