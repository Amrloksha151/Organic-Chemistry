Both contain the polar **Carbonyl Group ($C=O$)**.

## 1. Structure
- **Aldehydes ($R-CHO$):** Carbonyl at the **end**. Suffix *-al*.
- **Ketones ($R-CO-R'$):** Carbonyl in the **middle**. Suffix *-one*.

## 2. IUPAC Nomenclature

### A. Aldehydes (Suffix: -al)
- **Rule:** The carbonyl carbon is **always Carbon 1**. 
- **Naming:** No number is needed for the group position (e.g., *Ethanal*, *3-methylbutanal*).

### B. Ketones (Suffix: -one)
- **Rule:** Number from the end **closest to the carbonyl group**.
- **Naming:** Indicate the position for chains with 5+ carbons (e.g., *pentan-2-one*).

## 3. Properties
- **Polarity:** Higher BP than alkanes, but lower than alcohols (no intermolecular H-bonding).
- **Solubility:** Short chains are water-soluble due to H-bonding *with* water.

## 3. Synthesis
- **From Alcohols:**
  - $1^\circ \text{ Alcohol} \xrightarrow{PCC} \text{Aldehyde}$.
  - $2^\circ \text{ Alcohol} \xrightarrow{[O]} \text{Ketone}$.

## 4. Reactions
- **Nucleophilic Addition:** The $\delta^+$ Carbon is attacked by nucleophiles (e.g., $CN^-$).
- **Reduction:**
  - $\text{Aldehyde} + [H] \rightarrow 1^\circ \text{ Alcohol}$.
  - $\text{Ketone} + [H] \rightarrow 2^\circ \text{ Alcohol}$.
- **Oxidation:**
  - Aldehydes $\rightarrow$ Carboxylic Acids.
  - Ketones $\rightarrow$ No Reaction.
