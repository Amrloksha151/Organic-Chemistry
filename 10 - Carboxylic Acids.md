Defined by the **Carboxyl Group ($-COOH$)**.

## 1. IUPAC Nomenclature
- **Suffix:** *-oic acid*.
- **Priority:** The carboxyl carbon is **always Carbon 1** and takes priority over all other groups.
- **Common Names:** 
  - Methanoic acid = *Formic acid*.
  - Ethanoic acid = *Acetic acid*.

## 2. Properties
- **Highest BP:** Form stable **dimers** via two hydrogen bonds per pair of molecules.
- **Acidity:** Weak acids. Resonance stabilizes the carboxylate ion ($R-COO^-$).
- **Naming:** Suffix *-oic acid*. Carbon 1 is always the carboxyl carbon.

## 2. Synthesis
- **Oxidation:** $1^\circ \text{ Alcohol} \text{ or Aldehyde} \xrightarrow{K_2Cr_2O_7 / H^+} \text{Carboxylic Acid}$.

## 3. Reactions
- **Neutralization:** Acid + Base $\rightarrow$ Salt + Water.
- **Esterification:** Acid + Alcohol $\rightleftharpoons$ **Ester** + $H_2O$ (requires $H_2SO_4$ catalyst).
- **Reduction:** Requires $LiAlH_4$ to return to $1^\circ$ Alcohol.
