# 05 - Isomerism

Isomerism occurs when two or more compounds have the same molecular formula but different arrangements of atoms. It is broadly divided into two categories.

## 1. Structural (Constitutional) Isomerism
Molecules with the same formula but different **connectivity**.
- **Chain Isomers:** Different arrangements of the carbon skeleton (e.g., Pentane vs. Isopentane).
- **Position Isomers:** Functional group or multiple bond is in a different location (e.g., 1-butene vs. 2-butene).
- **Functional Isomers:** Different functional groups (e.g., Ethanol $CH_3CH_2OH$ and Dimethyl ether $CH_3OCH_3$).

## 2. Stereoisomerism
Molecules with the same connectivity but different **spatial arrangements**.

### A. Geometric (Cis-Trans) Isomerism
Occurs in alkenes and cycloalkanes due to restricted rotation.
- **Cis:** Identical groups on the same side.
- **Trans:** Identical groups on opposite sides.

### B. Optical Isomerism (Chirality)
Occurs when a molecule has a **Chiral Center** (a carbon atom bonded to four *different* groups).
- **Enantiomers:** Non-superimposable mirror images (like left and right hands).
- **Properties:** Enantiomers have identical physical properties (MP, BP) but rotate plane-polarized light in opposite directions.

### Worked Problem 5.1: Identifying Structural Isomers
**Question:** Draw and name all structural isomers of $C_4H_9Cl$.

**Solution:**
1. **1-chlorobutane:** $Cl-CH_2-CH_2-CH_2-CH_3$
2. **2-chlorobutane:** $CH_3-CH(Cl)-CH_2-CH_3$
3. **1-chloro-2-methylpropane:** $Cl-CH_2-CH(CH_3)_2$
4. **2-chloro-2-methylpropane:** $CH_3-C(Cl)(CH_3)_2$

### Worked Problem 5.2: Geometric Isomerism
**Question:** Which of the following can exist as cis-trans isomers?
a) 1-butene  
b) 2-butene  
c) 1,1-dichloroethene

**Solution:**
- **a) 1-butene:** ($CH_2=CHCH_2CH_3$) No. The first carbon has two identical H atoms.
- **b) 2-butene:** ($CH_3CH=CHCH_3$) **Yes**. Each carbon of the double bond is attached to a H and a methyl group.
- **c) 1,1-dichloroethene:** ($CCl_2=CH_2$) No. The first carbon has two identical Cl atoms.

---
### Worked Problem 5.3: Chiral Centers
**Question:** Identify the chiral carbon in 2-butanol ($CH_3-CH(OH)-CH_2-CH_3$).

**Solution:**
1. Examine C2.
2. It is bonded to:
   - A hydrogen atom ($-H$)
   - A hydroxyl group ($-OH$)
   - A methyl group ($-CH_3$)
   - An ethyl group ($-CH_2CH_3$)
3. Since all four groups are different, **C2 is a chiral center**.
