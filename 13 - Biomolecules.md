# Biomolecules

Organic chemistry is the foundation of biology. The two main classes of biomolecules often covered in introductory courses are **Carbohydrates** and **Proteins**.

## 1. Carbohydrates
Compounds made of Carbon, Hydrogen, and Oxygen ($C_n(H_2O)_m$). They serve as the primary energy source for life.

- **Monosaccharides:** Simple sugars (e.g., Glucose, Fructose).
- **Disaccharides:** Two sugars linked together (e.g., Sucrose = Glucose + Fructose).
- **Polysaccharides:** Long chains of sugars (e.g., Starch for energy storage, Cellulose for plant structure).

## 2. Proteins
Large, complex molecules that do most of the work in cells. They are **polymers of Amino Acids**.

### Amino Acids
Contain both an **Amine ($-NH_2$)** group and a **Carboxylic Acid ($-COOH$)** group.
- There are 20 standard amino acids that differ by their **R-group** (side chain).

### The Peptide Bond
Amino acids join via a **condensation reaction** between the acid group of one and the amine group of another, forming an **Amide link** (Peptide Bond).
- **Primary Structure:** The sequence of amino acids in the chain.
