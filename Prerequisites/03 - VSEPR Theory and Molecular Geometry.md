**VSEPR** stands for **Valence Shell Electron Pair Repulsion**. This theory allows us to predict the 3D shape of a molecule based on the idea that electron pairs (bonds and lone pairs) want to stay as far away from each other as possible to minimize repulsion.
<hr>
## 1. Steric Number
To determine the shape, first calculate the **Steric Number (SN)**:
$$\text{SN} = (\text{Number of atoms bonded to central atom}) + (\text{Number of lone pairs on central atom})$$

## 3. How to Predict Structure (Step-by-Step)
1. **Draw the Lewis Structure:** (See [[02 - Lewis Structures and Chemical Bonding]]).
2. **Count Electron Domains:** Treat double and triple bonds as **one** domain. Count lone pairs as one domain.
3. **Determine Electron Geometry:** Based on the total domains (Steric Number).
4. **Determine Molecular Geometry:** Adjust for the presence of lone pairs (lone pairs take up more space and "squeeze" the bond angles).

## 4. Connection to Hybridization
The geometry of a molecule is directly linked to the hybridization of its central atom:
- **Tetrahedral** geometry usually implies **$sp^3$** hybridization.
- **Trigonal Planar** geometry usually implies **$sp^2$** hybridization.
- **Linear** geometry usually implies **$sp$** hybridization.

These concepts are fundamental when discussing the physical properties and reactivity of molecules in [[01 - Basics and Definitions]].
