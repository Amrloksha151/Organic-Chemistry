Unsaturated hydrocarbons contain **$\pi$-bonds**, which are sites of high electron density and reactivity.

## 1. Structure
- **Alkenes ($C_nH_{2n}$):** Double bond ($1\sigma, 1\pi$). Trigonal planar ($120^\circ$).
- **Alkynes ($C_nH_{2n-2}$):** Triple bond ($1\sigma, 2\pi$). Linear ($180^\circ$).

## 2. IUPAC Nomenclature
Multiple bonds take priority over alkyl groups for numbering.

### A. General Rules
1. **Parent Chain:** MUST contain the double/triple bond. Suffix is **-ene** or **-yne**.
2. **Numbering:** Start from the end **closest to the multiple bond**.
3. **Position:** Use a number to indicate where the bond starts (e.g., *but-1-ene* or *2-pentyne*).
4. **Cycloalkenes:** The double bond is **always** carbons 1 and 2. Number in the direction that gives substituents the lowest numbers.

### B. Geometric (Cis-Trans) Isomerism
Because double bonds cannot rotate, spatial arrangement matters:
- **Cis-**: Identical groups on the **same side** of the bond.
- **Trans-**: Identical groups on **opposite sides** of the bond.
- *Example:* *cis-but-2-ene* vs. *trans-but-2-ene*.

## 3. Addition Reactions
The $\pi$-bond breaks to form two new $\sigma$-bonds.

| Reaction | Reagent | Product |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Hydrogenation** | $H_2 / Pt$ | Alkane |
| **Halogenation** | $Br_2$ or $Cl_2$ | Dihalide |
| **Hydrohalogenation** | $HBr$ or $HCl$ | Alkyl Halide |
| **Hydration** | $H_2O / H^+$ | Alcohol |

### Markovnikov's Rule
In the addition of $HX$ to an unsymmetrical alkene, the **$H$ attaches to the carbon with more $H$ atoms** (creating the most stable carbocation).

### Alkyne Specifics
- Alkynes can add reagents **twice** (e.g., Alkyne $\rightarrow$ Alkene $\rightarrow$ Alkane).
- **Lindlar's Catalyst** stops $H_2$ addition at the **Cis-alkene**.
- **Na / $NH_3$ (liq)** stops $H_2$ addition at the **Trans-alkene**.

## 4. Polymerization
Alkenes link together to form long chains (e.g., Ethene $\rightarrow$ Polyethene). See [[12 - Polymers]].
