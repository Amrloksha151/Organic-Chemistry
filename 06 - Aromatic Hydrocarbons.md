Aromatic compounds (**Arenes**) are stable cyclic molecules with delocalized $\pi$-electrons.

## 1. [[04 - Alkenes and Alkynes|Benzene]] ($C_6H_6$)
- **Structure:** Planar hexagon, $sp^2$ hybridized, $120^\circ$ angles.
- **Resonance:** The 6 $\pi$-electrons are delocalized (circle in the middle), providing extreme stability.
- **Naming:** 
  - Mono-substituted: *Nitrobenzene*, *Chlorobenzene*.
  - Di-substituted: Use **ortho (1,2)**, **meta (1,3)**, or **para (1,4)**.

## 2. IUPAC Nomenclature

### A. Monosubstituted
- Name the substituent followed by *benzene* (e.g., *Ethylbenzene*).
- **Common Names:** *Toluene* (Methyl-), *Phenol* (-OH), *Aniline* (-NH2).

### B. Disubstituted (Ortho, Meta, Para)
- **Ortho (o-):** 1,2-position.
- **Meta (m-):** 1,3-position.
- **Para (p-):** 1,4-position.
- *Example:* *p-dichlorobenzene*.

## 3. Electrophilic Aromatic Substitution (EAS)
Benzene undergoes **Substitution** (not addition) to keep its stable aromatic ring intact.

| Reaction | Reagents | Group Added |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Nitration** | $HNO_3 / H_2SO_4$ | $-NO_2$ |
| **Halogenation** | $Cl_2 / FeCl_3$ | $-Cl$ |
| **Sulfonation** | $SO_3 / H_2SO_4$ | $-SO_3H$ |
| **Alkylation** | $R-Cl / AlCl_3$ | $-R$ (Alkyl) |

## 3. Directing Effects
- **O/P Directors:** $-OH, -NH_2, -CH_3$, Halogens.
- **Meta Directors:** $-NO_2, -COOH, -SO_3H$.
