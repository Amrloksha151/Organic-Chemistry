# Polymers

Polymers are giant molecules (macromolecules) formed by joining thousands of small units called **monomers**.

## 1. Addition Polymerization
Occurs in **alkenes** where the double bond breaks to link monomers together. The polymer is the only product.
- **Polyethene:** From ethene (used in plastic bags).
- **Polypropene:** From propene (used in crates and ropes).
- **Polychloroethene (PVC):** From chloroethene (used in pipes).

## 2. Condensation Polymerization
Monomers join by eliminating a small molecule (usually **water** or $HCl$).
- **Polyesters:** Formed from a dicarboxylic acid and a diol.
- **Polyamides (Nylon):** Formed from a dicarboxylic acid and a diamine.

## 3. Natural Polymers
- **Proteins:** Polymers of amino acids (linked by amide/peptide bonds).
- **Carbohydrates:** Polymers of sugars (like starch and cellulose).
- **DNA:** Polymers of nucleotides.
