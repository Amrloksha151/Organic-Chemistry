# 01 - Basics and Definitions of Organic Chemistry

Organic chemistry is the study of carbon-containing compounds. Carbon's unique position in the periodic table allows it to form the complex molecules necessary for life.

## The Nature of Carbon
Carbon has the electron configuration $[He] 2s^2 2p^2$. To achieve a stable octet, it forms four covalent bonds. 
- **Bonding Capacity:** Carbon can form single, double, or triple bonds with itself and other elements (H, O, N, S, and halogens).
- **Catenation:** The ability of carbon to form long, stable chains and rings.
- **Electronegativity:** Carbon has an electronegativity of 2.5, which is intermediate, allowing it to form stable bonds with both electropositive and electronegative elements.

## Orbital Hybridization
According to Valence Bond Theory, carbon's atomic orbitals mix to form hybrid orbitals to facilitate bonding:
1. **$sp^3$ Hybridization:** 
   - One *s* and three *p* orbitals mix.
   - Forms 4 equivalent orbitals arranged tetrahedrally ($109.5^\circ$).
   - Found in [[03 - Alkanes|Alkanes]].
2. **$sp^2$ Hybridization:**
   - One *s* and two *p* orbitals mix; one *p* orbital remains unhybridized.
   - Forms 3 equivalent orbitals in a trigonal planar arrangement ($120^\circ$).
   - The unhybridized *p* orbital forms a **pi ($\pi$) bond**.
   - Found in [[04 - Alkenes and Alkynes#Alkenes|Alkenes]].
3. **$sp$ Hybridization:**
   - One *s* and one *p* orbital mix; two *p* orbitals remain unhybridized.
   - Forms 2 equivalent orbitals in a linear arrangement ($180^\circ$).
   - Found in [[04 - Alkenes and Alkynes#Alkynes|Alkynes]].

## Classification of Hydrocarbons
Hydrocarbons are divided based on their bonding:
- **Aliphatic:** Chains and non-aromatic rings.
    - **Saturated:** [[03 - Alkanes|Alkanes]] (single bonds).
    - **Unsaturated:** [[04 - Alkenes and Alkynes|Alkenes and Alkynes]] (multiple bonds).
- **Aromatic:** Based on the benzene ring structure.

### Worked Problem 1.1: Hybridization Identification
**Question:** Identify the hybridization of each carbon atom in the following molecule: $CH_2=CH-CH_3$ (Propene).

**Solution:**
1. **C1 ($CH_2$):** Attached to two H atoms and one double bond. It has 3 bonding regions (2 single, 1 double). Therefore, it is **$sp^2$ hybridized**.
2. **C2 ($CH$):** Attached to one H, one single bond, and one double bond. It has 3 bonding regions. Therefore, it is **$sp^2$ hybridized**.
3. **C3 ($CH_3$):** Attached to three H atoms and one single bond. It has 4 bonding regions. Therefore, it is **$sp^3$ hybridized**.
