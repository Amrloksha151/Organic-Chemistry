# Functional Groups

A **functional group** is a specific arrangement of atoms within a molecule that is responsible for the characteristic chemical reactions of that molecule. While the hydrocarbon "backbone" of an organic molecule is relatively inert, the functional group is the site of reactivity.

## 1. Overview of Common Functional Groups

| Class of Compound | Functional Group | Structure | Suffix/Prefix | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Alkanes** | Single bond | $C-C$ | -ane | Ethane |
| **Alkenes** | Double bond | $C=C$ | -ene | Ethene |
| **Alkynes** | Triple bond | $C\equiv C$ | -yne | Ethyne |
| **Haloalkanes** | Halogen | $-X$ ($Cl, Br, I$) | halo- | Chloromethane |
| **Alcohols** | Hydroxyl | $-OH$ | -ol | Ethanol |
| **Ethers** | Ether | $-O-$ | -oxy- / ether | Dimethyl ether |
| **Aldehydes** | Carbonyl (terminal) | $-CHO$ | -al | Ethanal |
| **Ketones** | Carbonyl (internal) | $-C(=O)-$ | -one | Propanone |
| **Carboxylic Acids** | Carboxyl | $-COOH$ | -oic acid | Ethanoic acid |
| **Esters** | Ester | $-COOR$ | -oate | Methyl ethanoate |
| **Amines** | Amino | $-NH_2$ | -amine | Methylamine |
| **Amides** | Amido | $-CONH_2$ | -amide | Ethanamide |

## 2. Oxygen-Containing Groups

### Alcohols ($-OH$)
Alcohols contain the **hydroxyl group**. They are classified based on the number of carbons attached to the carbon bearing the $-OH$:
- **Primary ($1^\circ$):** $R-CH_2OH$
- **Secondary ($2^\circ$):** $R_2-CHOH$
- **Tertiary ($3^\circ$):** $R_3-COH$

### Carbonyl Compounds (The $C=O$ group)
The carbonyl group is a carbon double-bonded to an oxygen. Its position defines the class:
- **Aldehydes:** The carbonyl is at the **end** of the carbon chain ($R-CHO$).
- **Ketones:** The carbonyl is in the **middle** of the carbon chain ($R-CO-R'$).

### Carboxylic Acids and Derivatives
- **Carboxylic Acids:** Contain both a carbonyl and a hydroxyl group on the same carbon ($R-COOH$). They are weak acids.
- **Esters:** Formed by the reaction of an alcohol and a carboxylic acid. They often have pleasant, fruity smells.

---

## 3. Nitrogen-Containing Groups

### Amines
Derivatives of ammonia ($NH_3$) where one or more hydrogen atoms are replaced by alkyl groups.
- They are basic in nature and often have strong, "fishy" odors.

### Amides
A combination of a carbonyl group and an amino group ($R-CONH_2$). This bond (the **peptide bond**) is the fundamental link in proteins.

---

## 4. Priority in IUPAC Naming
When a molecule contains more than one functional group, one is chosen as the "principal" group to determine the suffix, while others are treated as substituents (prefixes).

**Decreasing Priority Order:**
1. Carboxylic Acid (Highest)
2. Ester
3. Amide
4. Aldehyde
5. Ketone
6. Alcohol
7. Amine
8. Alkene / Alkyne
9. Halide / Alkyl (Lowest)
