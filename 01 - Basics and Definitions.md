Organic Chemistry is the study of **Carbon-based compounds**. Carbon's unique ability to form stable chains and rings (**catenation**) allows for the vast complexity of life.

## Why Carbon?
1. **Tetravalency:** Carbon has 4 valence electrons, allowing it to form 4 strong covalent bonds.
2. **Hybridization:** Carbon mixes its $s$ and $p$ orbitals ($sp^3, sp^2, sp$) to create specific 3D shapes:
   - **$sp^3$:** Tetrahedral ($109.5^\circ$) - Found in [[03 - Alkanes]].
   - **$sp^2$:** Trigonal Planar ($120^\circ$) - Found in [[04 - Alkenes and Alkynes#Alkenes|Alkenes]].
   - **$sp$:** Linear ($180^\circ$) - Found in [[04 - Alkenes and Alkynes#Alkynes|Alkynes]].

## Key Concepts
- **Hydrocarbons:** Compounds containing *only* Hydrogen and Carbon.
- **Functional Groups:** Specific clusters of atoms that determine a molecule's reactivity (see [[07 - Functional Groups]]).
- **Homologous Series:** A family of compounds with the same functional group and general formula, where each member differs by a $-CH_2-$ unit.
